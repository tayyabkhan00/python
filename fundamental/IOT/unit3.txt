MAC protocols in IoT control access to the shared wireless medium to reduce collisions and save energy. Due to resource 
constraints, IoT MAC protocols emphasize low power consumption, scalability, and reliability. Contention-based protocols 
like CSMA offer flexibility but suffer from collisions, while schedule-based protocols like TDMA provide collision-free 
communication at the cost of synchronization overhead. Energy-efficient MAC protocols such as S-MAC and T-MAC introduce 
sleepâ€“wake cycles to extend network lifetime. IEEE 802.15.4 is the most widely used MAC standard for IoT applications.
