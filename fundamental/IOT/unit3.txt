Wireless Medium Access Issues in IoT

Wireless medium access in IoT faces challenges such as collision, hidden node problem, limited bandwidth, high energy 
consumption, scalability issues, and interference. Since IoT devices are battery-powered and deployed in large numbers, 
efficient medium access mechanisms are required. These issues are addressed using energy-efficient MAC protocols, 
time-slot scheduling, duty cycling, and collision avoidance techniques.

What is Wireless Medium Access?
In IoT, many sensor nodes share the same wireless channel (air).
Medium Access defines how nodes decide when to transmit data so that:
Data is not lost
Energy is saved
Network remains stable

1. Collision Problem
What is Collision?
When two or more nodes transmit at the same time, their signals overlap â†’ data gets corrupted.

Why is collision serious in IoT?
Sensors have low power
Retransmission wastes battery
Reduces network lifetime
Example:
Node A and Node B send data simultaneously to Sink â†’ âŒ both packets lost

Impact:
Increased delay
More energy consumption
Lower throughput

2. Hidden Node Problem 
What is Hidden Node?
Two nodes cannot hear each other, but both send data to the same receiver, causing collision.
Example:
Node A ----> Sink <---- Node C
(A and C cannot hear each other)

Why common in IoT?
Low transmission power
Short radio range
Dense sensor deployment

4. Energy Consumption Issue (Most Critical in IoT)
Why energy is a big issue?

5. Scalability Issue
What is Scalability?
Ability to support thousands or millions of nodes

6. Bandwidth Limitation
Why limited bandwidth?

Low data-rate radios used to save energy

7. Interference Issue
